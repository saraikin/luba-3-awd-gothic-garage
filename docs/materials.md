# Materials

## Wood and structure

- 12 mm waterproof plywood.
- 30×30 mm or 40×40 mm timber battens.
- Outdoor-rated screws, approx. 4×35 or 4×40 mm.
- Stainless screws for exposed parts.
- D3/D4 wood glue or polyurethane adhesive.
- Exterior wood primer.
- Exterior paint or varnish.
- Edge sealer for plywood cuts.

## Roof

- Plywood roof base.
- Printed slate texture film, bitumen shingles, or thin plastic roof tiles.
- Drip edge or small overhang.
- Rubber seal if using a hinged roof.
- Magnets/latches/locating pins for removable roof.

## Facade

- Outdoor vinyl film with UV-resistant print.
- Optional matte laminate.
- Stone wall texture.
- Stained-glass window graphics.
- Roof texture graphics.
- Printed shadow lines for false floors and Gothic depth.

## Windows

Two possible approaches:

### Printed-only windows

- Simpler.
- More waterproof.
- Good for MVP.

### Real cut-out windows

- Better with internal light.
- Requires edge sealing.
- Use acrylic or polycarbonate behind the cut-out.
- Add LED diffuser film if needed.

## 3D printing

Recommended outdoor materials:

- PETG: easiest outdoor-friendly option.
- ASA: better UV/weather resistance, harder to print.

Avoid PLA for long-term outdoor use.

Suggested printed parts:

- Spires.
- Cross.
- Rose window frame.
- Gate arch.
- Columns.
- Buttresses.
- Erkers / bay windows.
- Roof finials.
- Vent grille covers.

## Electrical

- 12 V warm white LED strip.
- IP-rated LED strip where exposed.
- 12 V power supply, ventilated.
- Cable glands.
- Cable channel.
- Wago-style connectors or equivalent safe connectors.
- Fuse appropriate to LED load.
- Ventilation grille near power supply.

## Recommended fasteners and fittings

- Hinges for roof if hinged design is used.
- Magnetic catches or draw latches.
- Rubber feet or raised base blocks.
- Stainless angle brackets where needed.
- Small handles hidden under roof edge.
