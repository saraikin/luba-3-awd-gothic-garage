# Safety and weatherproofing

## Electrical safety

- Keep power supplies above floor level.
- Keep electrical components away from direct water paths.
- Add ventilation around power supply and LED controller.
- Use cable glands where cables pass through plywood.
- Provide strain relief for all cables.
- Do not trap the charging cable under the robot path.
- Use outdoor-rated components where exposure is possible.

## Ventilation

The garage must not become a sealed box.

Recommended:

- Low rear/side intake vent.
- High roof/service compartment exhaust vent.
- Hidden vents behind Gothic decor or printed grille graphics.

## Water protection

- Seal all plywood edges before assembly if possible.
- Apply 2–3 coats to exposed edges.
- Avoid water-trapping slots and horizontal grooves.
- Add roof overhang of 40–70 mm.
- Add a drip edge under roof overhang.
- Keep the floor slightly raised above ground.
- Ensure water cannot flow into the entrance.

## Robot movement safety

- Entrance threshold should be flat or very low.
- Keep screws, cables, and shelves outside the robot path.
- Test manual docking before adding decorative elements.
- Avoid internal side projections near the charging station.

## Maintenance access

- Roof should open or lift off without tools.
- Charging station should be removable without dismantling the garage.
- Power supply compartment should be accessible for inspection.
- Spare parts should not fall into the robot bay.

## Wind resistance

A decorative tall tower can catch wind.

Recommended:

- Keep tower lightweight.
- Attach tower to roof with screws from inside or hidden bolts.
- Lock removable roof with latches or magnets.
- Add weight or ground anchors if the site is exposed.
