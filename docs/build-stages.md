# Build stages

## Core architecture

The project uses two main modules:

1. **Lower functional module** — the actual working robot garage.
2. **Upper architectural module** — a hinged Gothic roof/fronton/tower assembly added later.

The build must be incremental and non-destructive:

- Every stage must be usable as a final stopping point.
- No completed part should need to be thrown away later.
- Later stages should add modules, printed film, or decorative parts on top of the previous stage.
- Printed facade film may be added per module and replaced per module if needed, but the goal is to avoid reworking the whole garage.

## Stage 1 — Lower functional module

Purpose: create a working garage that can already be used for docking and charging.

Build:

- Concrete slabs as the ground base.
- Timber batten frame on the slabs.
- No plywood floor; the robot stands on the slabs.
- Side walls.
- Rear wall.
- Front wall with practical low mower opening.
- Horizontal plywood ceiling above the robot bay.
- The ceiling also acts as a service shelf.
- Temporary weatherproof top surface on the ceiling: paint, waterproof coating, or temporary cover.

Service shelf use:

- Charging power supply.
- Future LED power supply.
- LED controller.
- Spare blades.
- Screws and small spare parts.
- Cleaning brush.
- Documentation in a sealed bag or box.

Important:

- The roof is not required at Stage 1.
- The horizontal ceiling is the temporary top cover.
- Do not add Gothic relief, columns, cornices, towers, or decorative projections at this stage.

Recommended checks:

- Robot enters and exits without touching the gate.
- Charging station remains aligned.
- Cable does not interfere with robot movement.
- The 1500 mm straight docking/undocking path remains clear in front of the station.
- Water cannot run directly into the robot bay.
- Service shelf is accessible from above.

## Stage 2 — Printed facade on the lower module

Purpose: make the lower module visually finished while keeping it flat and simple.

Add only printed outdoor vinyl film:

- Stone wall graphics.
- Practical gate graphic.
- Printed first-row false windows.
- Printed shadows and alignment marks.
- Optional printed ventilation grille graphics.

Do not add physical relief at this stage.

Rules:

- The surface remains flat.
- Windows are printed, not cut, unless specifically needed later.
- The lower module should look complete even if no upper module is ever added.

## Stage 3 — Hinged upper architectural module

Purpose: add the Gothic church silhouette without modifying or rebuilding the lower module.

Build a separate hinged upper module:

- Gable roof.
- Front gable/fronton.
- Rear gable/fronton.
- Ridge beam or roof frame.
- Rear hinges.
- Opening support/prop or limiter.
- Drip edge / overlap over the lower box.

This upper module is attached to the lower module with hinges and opens as one piece.

Important:

- The fronton is part of the upper module, not the lower wall.
- The lower module facade does not need to be replaced.
- The service shelf remains below the upper module.
- The upper module should be removable/repairable as a separate assembly if needed.

## Stage 4 — Printed facade on the upper module

Purpose: make the roof and frontons visually complete.

Add outdoor vinyl film to the upper module:

- Slate/tile roof graphics.
- Printed upper false-window rows on the fronton.
- Printed rose window.
- Printed Gothic lines and shadows.
- Rear-fronton graphics if desired.

The upper module should look complete at this stage even without towers.

## Stage 5 — Central tower module

Purpose: add the main vertical Gothic element as a separate upgrade.

Build:

- Simple plywood tower box.
- Small tower roof.
- Mounting base on the hinged upper module.
- Optional internal cable route for future LED.
- Separate printed film for the tower.

Important:

- The tower belongs to the upper module, not the lower box.
- When the roof opens, the tower opens with it.
- No part of the lower module needs to be cut or rebuilt.
- The tower may be added later, or skipped entirely.

## Stage 6 — Corner tower modules

Purpose: add the four smaller corner towers as optional architectural upgrades.

Build:

- Four small plywood tower boxes.
- Small individual roofs.
- Mounting points on the upper module.
- Separate printed film for each tower.

Important:

- Corner towers are optional.
- They should not block the hinged opening movement.
- They should not require changes to the lower module facade.

## Stage 7 — Optional physical decor

Purpose: add depth only if there is time and interest.

Possible additions:

- 3D-printed columns.
- Cornices.
- Arches.
- Spires.
- Cross.
- Rose window frame.
- Real cut-out stained-glass windows.
- LED backlighting.
- Decorative vent covers.

Rules:

- Physical decor is optional.
- It should be added as replaceable modules.
- It should not be required for the garage to look complete.
- Prefer PETG or ASA for outdoor 3D-printed parts.
- Avoid PLA for long-term outdoor use.

## Stage 8 — Refinement and community variants

Later project additions:

- Alternative dimensions for other Luba models.
- CNC-ready version.
- Laser-cut facade version.
- Parametric OpenSCAD/Fusion model.
- Multiple visual styles: Gothic chapel, castle gate, cottage, modern shed.
